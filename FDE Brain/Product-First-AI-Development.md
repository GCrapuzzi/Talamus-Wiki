# Product-First AI Development

## Summary

A decision framework for initiating AI projects that mandates defining the desired product outcome and user experience before committing resources to data collection or model training.

## Core Idea

The availability of powerful, pre-trained foundation models (FMs) fundamentally changes the risk profile of AI projects. Instead of treating data and models as prerequisites, they are treated as variables that must be optimized *after* the product concept has been validated. This minimizes sunk costs associated with poorly defined models.

## Practical Use

Before starting a project, ask: 'What is the minimum viable product (MVP) that delivers value?' This forces the team to define success metrics based on user interaction and business value, rather than model metrics (e.g., F1 score).

# Foundation Models (FMs)

## Summary

Large, general-purpose models trained on vast, diverse datasets, capable of being adapted to a wide range of downstream tasks with minimal task-specific training.

## Core Idea

FMs act as powerful, general-purpose bases, shifting the focus from building models from scratch (traditional ML) to adapting and orchestrating existing, highly capable systems. This significantly lowers the barrier to entry for complex AI applications.

## Practical Use

When starting a new project, assess if an existing FM can be leveraged via prompt engineering or fine-tuning, rather than attempting to train a model from zero. Understanding the FM's inherent capabilities and limitations is key to system design.

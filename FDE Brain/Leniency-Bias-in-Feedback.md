# Leniency Bias in Feedback

## Summary

The tendency for users to rate items more positively than warranted, often to avoid conflict or because providing negative feedback requires too much effort.

## Core Idea

Users may feel compelled to give high ratings (e.g., five stars) to minimize friction or effort, even if the experience was mediocre or poor. This skews the perceived quality of the system.

## Practical Use

When designing feedback mechanisms, avoid forcing users into simple star ratings. Instead, use descriptive, non-numerical options (e.g., 'Great ride. Great driver.' vs. 'Pretty good.') to allow for more nuanced and honest input.

# Scaling Hypothesis

The empirical observation that scaling up model size, data, and compute reliably improves model performance. Demonstrated as early as the 2012 AlexNet paper, which noted results could be "improved simply by waiting for faster GPUs and bigger datasets." This insight drove the foundation model era: GPT-series models at OpenAI were built on this premise.

The non-obvious corollary: a small increase in model quality metrics can produce a **non-linear explosion** in downstream applications. The capability jump from GPT-3 to ChatGPT-class models didn't just improve existing use cases—it unlocked entirely new application categories and dramatically lowered the entry barrier for developers.

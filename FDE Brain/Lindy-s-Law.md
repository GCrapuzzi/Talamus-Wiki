# Lindy's Law

A heuristic for technology longevity: the future life expectancy of a non-perishable technology is proportional to its current age. If a technique has survived N years, expect it to survive roughly another N years.

In AI engineering, Lindy's Law helps prioritize which techniques to invest in learning deeply vs. which are likely transient. Retrieval technology (decades old) underpins RAG and is likely durable. A specific prompt-formatting trick from last month may not be.

Useful as a filter when deciding whether to build on a technique or wait for it to stabilize.
